# Algorithmic Proof

Algorithmic Proof is a model of trust where the client’s AI, when asked, generates a coherent and consistent answer about a business, product or service.

---

## The shift

For years, trust in marketing has been based on social proof:

- reviews  
- testimonials  
- case studies  

This model relies on what a company shows.

Algorithmic Proof changes the dynamic.

The user does not rely only on visible proof.  
They ask their AI.

The answer becomes the reference.

---

## How it works

Algorithmic Proof is not random.

It is based on two elements:

### 1. The question

Different questions lead to different answers.

A vague question produces uncertainty.  
A structured question produces a structured answer.

The way the question is formulated directly influences the output.

---

### 2. The system behind the answer

The response generated by an AI depends on how information is structured and connected.

This includes:

- clarity of content  
- consistency of meaning  
- relationships between concepts  
- semantic structure of the system  

The goal is not to control the answer.

The goal is to make a specific answer the most natural outcome.

---

## Key idea

You don’t control what the AI will say.

But you can reach this point:

> you don’t know exactly what it will say,  
> but you know it won’t produce a negative or incoherent response.
